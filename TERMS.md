# Rules

## Application Rules

- You cannot apply to more than ONE buildathon.
- Choose the region appropriate to where you live or the one you hold a permanent residency for. If you win, you will be asked to verify your identity using these proofs.
- If you apply to more than one region and/or are found to be submitting multiple projects, your submission will be considered invalid.
- You can only be a part of ONE team at the buildathon.
- If you are found to spread hate or use offensive language at any point during the buildathon (or matters around this), we will have to unfortunately ban you from the platform. At no point, we tolerate being inappropriate with the team members and not respecting the terms of the buildathon.

## Project Submission Rules

- You can only submit ONE project to the buildathon. If you are found to be creating multiple accounts for submitting projects, all your submissions will be disqualified during the community review period and any bounty won by you will be ineligible for a prize.
- You can only submit one project for the buildathon. At any point, it has been found you have fabricated multiple accounts or have submitted multiple projects from one account, all your submissions and winnings will be ineligible.
- If your project is found to be Spam/Recycled or Plagiarized during the Community Review period, you will be asked to justify why your project is not in violation and to submit a legitimate submission. In case of insufficient claims or material provided, the project will be disqualified under review and any applicable prizes or bounties will be withheld.

## What qualifies as a valid project for the Base Batches?

In order to be considered as a valid project for Base Batches, the project:

- Must submit one project per team.
- Must include a functioning on-chain app at a publicly accessible URL.
- Must have an open-source GitHub repository for all source code in the app (showcase track projects are exempt).
- Must include a video demo, with a minimum length of 1 minute (Intro, Demo, Problem Statement, Closing).
- Must be deployed and live with Base.
- Must focus on building applications or infrastructure that is compatible with or for Base.
- Apps that focus on non-EVM chains will be disqualified.
- The app must be deployed to a publicly accessible URL (e.g., testnet/mainnet on Base).
- The app must be live for the judges to test.
- Projects deployed only to local environments will not be judged and will be disqualified.
- Only Projects submitted via the Video Editing Track: Must submit a video on chain.
- For the Video Editing Track, projects may be off-chain but must be deployed on platforms of the track's choice and submitted in the GDrive submission form shared with the team.
- All submissions must adhere to the terms in the Base Code of Conduct.
